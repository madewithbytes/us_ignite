GCTC 2016 – Participation Guide


Thank you for your interest in joining the Global City Teams Challenge (GCTC). Please review this Guide as it provides information regarding the following:

  1. Requirements for participation in the 2016 GCTC Expo (June 13-14 in Austin, TX) www.gctcexpo.org
  2. Process to register one or more new Action Clusters
  3. Potential funding opportunities
  4. GCTC timelines

GCTC Expo 2016 Participation Requirements:


To participate in the GCTC 2016 Expo, we ask that all Action Cluster provide the following information by April 29, 2016:

  1. A brief description of what your Action Cluster will present/exhibit at the GCTC Expo. Please submit your description form here. Please note: we request that your brief description adhere to a 100-word maximum.
  2. Name all of individuals participating in your Action Cluster. As appropriate, this should include the names, titles and contact information for the representatives of the municipal partners, universities, nonprofits, and/or corporations) participating in your Action Cluster. Please identify the individuals who will represent your Action Cluster at the GCTC Expo in June. You can submit your team list here.
  3. If you have trouble accessing the forms, please email Mariya Cherk at Mariya.Cherk@US-Ignite.org
  4. Reminder: At the GCTC Expo, an Action Cluster’s municipal government partner should be a part of the team presentation. Through its presentation and exhibition, an Action Cluster should demonstrate the feasibility of their project by exhibiting prototypes, pilots, and/or preliminary results.

Registering a New Action Cluster


To participate in GCTC, each new Action Cluster must complete and submit a project worksheet. 2015 GCTC Action Clusters continuing into the 2016 program must update and submit their previous project worksheet. GCTC organizers will then review the worksheet and send feedback to the team lead. When GCTC and the team lead agree on a final worksheet, the Action Cluster will be officially created. Each Action Cluster will be given::

  1. A free exhibition/demonstration space at the Expo (June 13-14) and a speaking slot. The priorities of the speaking slot will be given to the teams that show working demonstrations and/or pilot deployments at the Expo as part of the exhibition
  2. Opportunities for media coverage at the Expo
  3. Opportunities to collaborate with additional cities and smart city leaders in government, industry, and academia

The requirements to form an Action Cluster are as follows:

  1. Each team needs to have more than 1 entity.
  2. Each team should have at least one municipal government participant, which will deploy the solution. (At least one city official should actively participate in the team.)
  3. At the GCTC Expo in June 2016, the municipal government partner should participate and be part of the team presentation. Each team should demonstrate the feasibility of their project at the Expo by exhibiting prototypes, pilots, and/or preliminary results.
  4. Each team should plan to deploy and report the project’s measurable/quantifiable impact to the residents by June 2017 and demonstrate the result at the 2017 Expo.
  5. Optional: Teams are encouraged to have multiple municipal partners deploying the same project.

The worksheet template can be downloaded from here. A fictitious sample worksheet is here.


GCTC 2016 Action Cluster Deadlines


In order to present and exhibit at the 2016 Expo, an Action Cluster must submit the following by April 29:

  • Project worksheet for the the Action Custer (New Action Cluster Only)
  • Team Member List. Please use this form
  • Brief Description of what your Action Cluster will present at the Expo. Please use this form

Please note that the contents of these materials will be made public, except for the contact information of the team members other than the Action Cluster team lead.

Contact Mariya.Cherk@US-Ignite.org if you have trouble accessing any of these forms or if you have any questions.

    • Applications are due April 15th, 2016.
    • Info: https://www.us-ignite.org/globalcityteams/leadership-fund/
  • NIST Replicable Smart City Technologies (RSCT) Grant Program
    • For US local governments participating in the GCTC.
    • Three $100,000 awards for US local governments participating in the GCTC.
    • The application deadline is May 12, 2016.

GCTC Timeline:


Phase I: Team Building, November 2015 – June 2016


GCTC Kick-off Conference (November 12-13, 2015)

  • Webcast replay
  • Additional presentations

Tech Jam (March 22-23, 2016, Gaithersburg, MD)

  • Webcast replay of 2016 Tech Jam
  • Presentations from 2016 Tech Jam

Phase I Expo / Phase II Kick-off (June 13-14, 2016, Austin Convention Center, Austin, Texas, United States)

  • Featuring exhibitions and project presentations from Action Clusters
  • Opportunities to engage with other Action Clusters and Smart City leaders from industry, government, and academia

  • Projects should be deployed, and Action Clusters are encouraged to identify additional municipal partners to join the Cluster

Tech Jam (Q4 2016, tentative)

  • Action Clusters meet again to discuss the transition to project deployment phase and best practices for creating replicable, scalable, and sustainable Smart City models

April 2017

  • Action Clusters must substantially complete project implementation and measure success according to KPIs
  • Prepare demonstration for Expo

Phase II Expo (June 2017)

  • Action Clusters meet with Smart City leaders to demonstrate projects and promote replication elsewhere
  • Exceptional Action Clusters will receive special recognition
  • Visit www.gctcexpo.org for more information.
