GCTC 2016 – Participation Guide


Thank you for your interest in joining the Global City Teams Challenge (GCTC). There are a few different ways to participate in GCTC.

  1. Register one or more action clusters (i.e. teams) to present and demonstrate in the upcoming events (Tech Jam and GCTC Expo)
  2. Become a Partner of GCTC
  3. Participate as audience

This page explains how to register action clusters. Each action cluster must complete and submit a project worksheet. 2015 GCTC action clusters continuing into the 2016 program must update and submit their previous project worksheet. GCTC organizers will then review the worksheet and send feedback to the team lead. When GCTC and the team lead agree on a final worksheet, the action cluster will be officially created. Each action cluster will be given:

  1. a dedicated speaking slot at the Tech Jam (late March 2016)
  2. a free exhibition/demonstration space at the Expo (June 2016) and a speaking slot. The priorities of the speaking slot will be given to the teams that show working demonstrations and/or pilot deployments at the Expo as part of the exhibition
  3. opportunities for substantial media coverage at the Expo
  4. opportunities to collaborate with additional cities and smart city leaders in government, industry, and academia

The requirements to form a team are:

  1. Each team needs to have more than 1 entity.
  2. Each team should have at least one municipal government participant which will deploy the solution. (At least one city official should actively participate in the team.)
  3. At the Expo in June 2016, the municipal government partner should participate and be part of the team presentation. Each team should demonstrate feasibility of their project at the Expo by exhibiting prototypes, pilots, and/or preliminary results.
  4. Each team should plan to deploy and report the project’s measurable/quantifiable impact to the residents by June 2017 and demonstrate the result at the 2017 Expo.
  5. Optional: Teams are encouraged to have multiple municipal partners deploying the same project.

The worksheet template can be downloaded from here. A fictitious sample worksheet is here.


GCTC 2016 Action Cluster Deadlines


Due February 26, 2016 (to be considered for Tech Jam presentation)
To be considered for a March Tech Jam presentation, the project worksheet must be received by February 26, 2016.

  • Contact leads or Mariya.Cherk@us-ignite.org to join an existing Action Cluster (project team), or form a new Action Cluster by submitting a project worksheet to William.Maguire@us-ignite.org and Sokwoo.Rhee@nist.gov

Due April 29, 2016 (to be considered for 2016 Expo presentation and exhibition)

  • Final project worksheets for all clusters to be considered for the Expo
  • Project plan, which includes:
    1. list of all Action Cluster participants and contact information
    2. details on all municipal partner(s)
    3. Key Performance Indicators (KPIs) for each project, detailing how success of the project will be measured
    4. Project timeline
    5. Demonstrated commitment of resources required for project

Additional resources and timeline:


Phase I: Team Building, November 2015 – June 2016


GCTC Kick-off Conference (November 12-13, 2015)

  • Webcast replay
  • Additional presentations
  • IBM is hosting GCTC local kick-off events through February 2016

Tech Jam (late-March, Washington, D.C)

  • Action Clusters present their initial plans, identify additional partners, and receive feedback
  • If possible, city official(s) from each cluster should participate in the Tech Jam
  • 2015 Tech Jam overview

Phase I Expo / Phase II Kick-off (June 2016, United States)

  • Featuring exhibitions and project presentations from Action Clusters
  • Opportunities to engage with other Action Clusters and Smart City leaders from industry, government, and academia
  • The city official(s) in each cluster should join the Expo and participate in the cluster presentation

Phase II: Implementation, June 2016 – June 2017

Q3 2016

  • Projects should be deployed, and Action Clusters are encouraged to identify additional municipal partners to join the Cluster

Tech Jam (Q4 2016, tentative)

  • Action Clusters meet again to discuss the transition to project deployment phase and best practices for creating replicable, scalable, and sustainable Smart City models

April 2017

  • Action Clusters must substantially complete project implementation and measure success according to KPIs
  • Prepare demonstration for Expo

Phase II Expo (June 2017)

  • Action Clusters meet with Smart City leaders to demonstrate projects and promote replication elsewhere
  • Exceptional Action Clusters will receive special recognition
